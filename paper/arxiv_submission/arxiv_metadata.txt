TITLE:
QFL: A Production-Ready Quantum Federated Learning Framework
with EU AI Act Compliance and Differential Privacy

AUTHORS:
Ali Pourrahim
Centria University of Applied Sciences
Kokkola, Finland
ali.pourrahim@centria.fi

ABSTRACT (250 words max):
We present QFL, the first open-source, production-ready middleware for
Quantum Federated Learning (QFL) designed with EU AI Act and GDPR
compliance as first-class requirements. QFL integrates three independently
active research areas: (i) quantum cryptography via BB84 Quantum Key
Distribution (QKD) for secure model weight transmission, (ii) federated
learning with a Quantum-enhanced FedAvg (q-FedAvg) aggregation algorithm,
and (iii) a regulatory compliance engine providing immutable EU AI Act
Article 9 audit trails, (epsilon,delta)-differential privacy with Renyi
accounting, and distribution-free conformal prediction bounds on global
model accuracy. Unlike prior work that operates exclusively in simulation,
QFL is designed for real IBM Quantum hardware (IBM Brisbane, 127-qubit
Eagle r3) via the Primitives (SamplerV2) API. We benchmark against
classical FedAvg on MNIST (n=10,000) under non-IID Dirichlet splits
(alpha=0.5) over 20 rounds across six algorithm-privacy configurations.
Our results show that both FedAvg and q-FedAvg achieve 100% peak accuracy
(no-DP, rounds to 90%: 7), with graceful degradation under strict privacy
(epsilon=1.0: 75.0% for FedAvg, 76.2% for q-FedAvg, 20.0 total epsilon
consumed). q-FedAvg consistently outperforms FedAvg under DP constraints,
consistent with its fairness-aware aggregation. Communication overhead is
2.44 MB per round for a 101,770-parameter MLP across 3 clients. The
complete framework — including Kubernetes deployment, GitHub Actions CI/CD,
and EU AI Act compliance engine — is released as open-source software with
100% test coverage.
